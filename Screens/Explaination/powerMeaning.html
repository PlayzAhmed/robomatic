What is power?

In math, "power" is like a special way of saying "times." Imagine you have a superhero number, let's call it 'A' and it has a superpower represented by another number, 'N' When we write a to the power of 'N' or an, it means that our superhero number 'A' is going to use its superpower 'N' times.

For Example if 'A' is 2 and 'N' is 3, it's like saying our superhero number 2 is going to use its superpower 3 times. So, 23 means 2 times 2 times 2, which equals 8.
In simple terms, 'A'to the power of 'N' is just telling our superhero number to show off its power by multiplying itself 'N' times!

Strategies to use in Powers

Repeated Multiplication Strategy:

• 2³ means 2 raised to the power of 3, which is 2 × 2 × 2 = 8
• 5² means 5 raised to the power of 2, which is 5 × 5 = 25
