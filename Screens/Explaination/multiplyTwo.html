Multiplying 2 Digits

Remember that each digit has its own special place. The 'ones' are like the little helpers, and the 'tens' are the mighty leaders. Remember their places, and you'll conquer any math quest!


Strategies to use in Multiplying 2 Digits

Algorithm for Multiplying Two Numbers:

Start: Get two numbers to multiply.

Step 1: Write down the first number.
Step 2: Write down the second number below the first.
Step 3: Multiply the digits in the rightmost column (ones place). Write down the result below.
Step 4: If the result is 10 or more, carry the extra digit to the next column on the left.
Step 5: Repeat steps 3-4 for each column, moving one place to the left each time
Step 6: Add all the partial results to get the final product.
End: The final result is the product of the two numbers.

For Example: let's multiply 12 by 3

first multiply 3 by 2 it is 6 write it down,
then multiply 3 by 1 it is 3 write it down


So, the algorithm guided us through the process of multiplying 12 and 3, resulting in the answer 36


Area model for multiplaying two digits

Let's imagine you want to multiply 23 by 14.

First break down each number into its tens and ones: 20 + 3 and 10 + 4.

Create a grid with two rows and two columns.
Multiply the numbers in each box of the grid and add the results.
Add up the numbers in each box to get the final answer.
It's like arranging the multiplication in a grid to make it easier to calculate step by step.
