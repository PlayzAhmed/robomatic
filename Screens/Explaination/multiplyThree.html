Multiplcation By 3 Digits

Remember that each digit has its own special place. The "ones" are like the little helpers, the "tens" are the cool sidekicks, and the "hundreds" are the mighty leaders. Remember their places, and you'll conquer any math quest!

Stratigies to use in multiplying 3 Digits

1 - Area model :

Set Up the Grid :
 Draw a large rectangle and divide it into three vertical sections for each digit in the first number (234) and three horizontal sections for each digit in the second number (567). This creates a grid of nine smaller rectangles.

 Label the Sections :
 Write the digits in their respective sections. For example, 200 in the left section, 30 in the middle, and 4 in the right for the number 234. Similarly, write 500, 60, and 7 in the horizontal sections for 567.

 Multiply :
 Multiply the digits in each section. For instance, in the upper left section, multiply 2 (from 234) by 5 (from 567) to get 10. Write the result in this section. Repeat this process for all sections.

 Add :
 Finally, add up all the results in each small rectangle to get the final product.


This method visually breaks down the multiplication process and helps understand how each digit contributes to the final answer.

2 - Split Strategy:

Let's say you want to multiply by split 345 by 678:

 Think in parts:

Break down 345 into 300, 40, and 5.
Break down 678 into 600, 70, and 8.
Multiply each part:

Multiply the first set of numbers (300, 40, 5) by each number in the second set (600, 70, 8).
Add all those multiplication results together, Combine the results to get the final answer.

So, 354 x 678 = 233910
Remember, take it step by step, and it becomes much easier!

3 - Algorithm:

Start: Get two numbers to multiply.

Step 1: Write down the first number.
Step 2: Write down the second number below the first.
Step 3: Multiply the digits in the rightmost column (ones place). Write down the result below.
Step 4: If the result is 10 or more, carry the extra digit to the next column on the left.
Step 5: Repeat steps 3-4 for each column, moving one place to the left each time
Step 6: Add all the partial results to get the final product.
End: The final result is the product of the two numbers.

For Example: let's multiply 235 by 645

First Step : multiply 5 by 5 it is 25 write the ones (5) down and carry up the tens (2),
Second Step : multiply 5 by 3 it is 15 add the carried 2 it becomes 17 write down 7 and carry up 1
Third Step : multiply 5 by 2 it its 10 add the carried 1 it is 11 write it down
Fourth Step : shift left digit and multiply 4 by 5 it is 20 write down 0 and carry up 2
Fifth Step : multiply 4 by 3 it is 12 add the carried 2 it becomes 14 write 4 down and carry up 1
Sixth Step : multiply 4 by 2 it is 8 add the carried 1 it becomes 9 write it down
Seventh Step : shift left extra digit and multipy 6 by 5 it is 30 write 0 down and carry up 3 multiply 6 by the remaining digits then add the the products together


So, the algorithm guided us through the process of multiplying 235 and 645, resulting in the answer 151575
