Dividing 3 Digits

Remember that each digit has its own special place. The 'ones' are like the little helpers, the 'tens' are the cool sidekicks, the 'hundreds' are the heroes, and the 'thousands' are the mighty leaders. Remember their places, and you'll conquer any math quest!

Stratigies to use in Dividing 3 Digits


Using Algorithm to divide :

Let's divide 384 by 6.

Step onesee how many times 6 can go into the first digit, 3. It's 0 times because 6 is larger than 3. then write 0 over 3

Step 2Bring down the next digit, 8 making it 38 Now, see how many times 6 can go into 38. It's 6 times.
6 x 6 = 36 Write 6 over 8
Subtract 36 from 38 leaving 2 Compare 2 < 6

Step 3 Bring down the next digit, 4, making it 24 Now, see how many times 6 can go into 28 It's 4 times.
6 x 4 = 24.
Subtract 24 from 24 leaving 0

The final result is 64 and there's no remainder

Using Split Strategy to divide

Example: 456 ÷ 3

Split the Numbers: Break down 456 into smaller parts: 400 + 50 + 6.
Divide Each Part: Start with the biggest part (400) and see how many times 3 can fit into it. Then move to the next part (50), and lastly, the smallest part (6).
Combine the Answers: Put together the answers you got for each part.

For 400 ÷ 3 you get 133 with a remainder of 1
For 50 ÷ 3 you get 16 with a remainder of 2
For 6 ÷ 3 you get 2 with no remainder.

Final Result: Combine the answers

Answers 133 + 16 + 2 = 151
Remainders 2 - 1 = 1
So 456 ÷ 3 = 151 + 1 = 152

It's like breaking a big problem into smaller pieces and solving each piece!

2-Using Area Model to divide

Imagine you have a toy farm with fields. One field has 123 flowers, and another field has 456 butterflies.

Draw the fields: Draw two big rectangles, one for each field.

Divide each field: Break each rectangle into three parts - one for hundreds, one for tens, and one for units. So, you have three sections in each rectangle.
Plant the flowers and butterflies: In each section, write down the number of flowers or butterflies for that part. For example, in the "hundreds" section, write 100 for flowers and 400 for butterflies.

Multiply: Multiply the numbers in each corresponding section. For instance, in the "hundreds" section, you would multiply 100 by 400.

Add up the results: After multiplying all the parts, add up the results to find out how many flowers and butterflies you have in total.

It's like planting flowers and butterflies in different parts of your farm and then counting how many you have altogether!
