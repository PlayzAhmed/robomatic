Division 2 Digits

Remember that each digit has its own special place. The "ones" are like the little helpers, and the "tens" are the mighty leaders. Remember their places, and you'll conquer any math quest!

Strategies to use in Dividing 2 Digits

Using Algorithm to divide:

Imagine you have 36 candies, and you want to share them equally among 4 friends.

Give Some:You start by giving each friend as many candies as you can. You give the first friend0 candies because 0 times 4 is 0.

How Many Left?: After that, you see how many candies are left. Since you gave 0 candies, you still have all 36 left.

Next Friend: Now, you bring in the next friend, and you do the same. How many candies can you give each friend without having any left? That's 9 candies each.

36 4=9, and everyone gets an equal share!



Using spliting to divide

Imagine,you have the number 68.

Step 1:Look at the tens place, which is 60.

Step 2:Look at the units place, which is 8.

Now, you can do things with these parts, like maybe sharing the 60 between friends and then adding the 8 later. It's like breaking down a big number into two smaller ones to make things easier!

Equal groups:

Imagine you have a bunch of candies, and you want to share them equally among your friends. Repeated subtraction is like giving each friend as many candies as possible until you can't give any more. The remainder is what's left, the few candies that couldn't be shared equally.

Repeated subtraction:

Repeated subtraction is a method of dividing a number by repeatedly subtracting another number until you can't subtract anymore. Here's an example:

Let's say you have 15 cookies, and you want to share them equally among 4 friends using repeated subtraction:

Subtract 4 from 15: 15 - 4 = 11
Subtract 4 from 11: 11 - 4 = 7
Subtract 4 from 7: 7 - 4 = 3

Now, you can't subtract 4 anymore without going below 0. The remainder is 3, which means each friend gets 3 cookies, and there are 3 cookies left that couldn't be shared equally.
