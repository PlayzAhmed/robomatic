Dividing By 1 Digit

Division in math is the process of splitting a number into equal parts, it may has a remainder which is left when the number can't be divided evenly. It's like sharing cookies with friends the division tells you how many each person gets, and the remainder is the extra cookies you have after everyone gets their fair share.

Strategies to use in Division

Repeated subtraction :

It is a method of dividing a number by repeatedly subtracting another number until you can't subtract anymore.

Here's an example:

Frist,Subtract 4 from 15: 15 - 4 = 11
Second,Subtract 4 from 11: 11 - 4 = 7
Third, Subtract 4 from 7: 7 - 4 = 3 Now, you can't subtract 4 anymore without going below 0. The remainder is 3, which means each friend gets 3 cookies ( number of subtracting steps ) , and there are 3 cookies left that couldn't be shared equally.

Equal groups:

It means in math making sure each bunch of things has the same number. Like if you have 12 candies and want to share with 3 friends, you make 4 equal groups, giving each friend the same amount of candies.
